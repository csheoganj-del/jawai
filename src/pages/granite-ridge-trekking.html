The Aravali Outcrops

Traverse the rocky hills that shelter Jawai's secret wildlife.

Jawai\'s striking hills are geologically unique. They consist of pre-Cambrian granite formations that are more than a billion years old, shaped by wind, rain, and tectonic shifts over eons. These rocks feature high domes, deep ravines, and natural hollow caves that provide the perfect denning shelters for leopards, striped hyenas, and nesting raptors.

Our guided treks offer an active, immersive way to explore this dry mountain ecosystem. Accompanied by a climbing naturalist, you will hike up gentle stone inclines, climb high viewpoints, and discover ancient temples and hermitages hidden inside cave vaults.

Trek Highlights & Trails

The Cave Temple of Devgiri

Trek up to the sacred shrine of Devgiri, carved naturally inside a giant hollow monolith. The trail winds through rocky crevices and wild cactus beds. At the temple, learn about the local folklore and watch how the shepherds and big cats share this sacred cave peace in absolute harmony.

The Ridge Skyline Walk

Follow the spine of a massive granite outcrop overlooking the Jawai Bandh reservoir. This trail offers panoramic views of the grasslands, marshlands, and waterbody below. It is the perfect spot for spotting migratory waterbirds, crocodiles basking on sandbanks, and raptors riding warm air currents.

Private Ridge Sundowner

At the end of the trail, arrive at a high cliff viewpoint where a private outpost butler awaits. Relax on luxury cushions and watch the desert sun dip below the horizon, bathing the valley in golden, purple, and crimson hues, while enjoying a glass of fine champagne and warm, chef-prepared appetizers.

Common Questions

Trekking FAQ

What kind of footwear should I wear?

Sturdy trail running shoes or hiking boots with good rubber grip are essential. The granite rock surfaces are generally clean but can be steep and sandy in places, so proper traction is key to hiking safely.

Are there leopards around when we trek?

Yes, leopards live in the hills, but they are naturally shy, nocturnal animals that rest deep inside caves during the day. Our naturalists monitor leopard movements constantly, map safe routes, and maintain complete field safety protocols throughout the trek.

What is the best time of day for trekking?

Treks are organized either at early dawn (06:00 AM) to catch the cool air and sunrise or in the late afternoon (04:00 PM) to align with our signature sunset sundowner experience.
