How to Reach Jawai: Flight, Train, and Road Directions

A comprehensive transportation guide to Jawai Bandh, Pali. Route options from Udaipur, Jodhpur, Jaipur, Delhi, and Mumbai.

Situated in the rugged hills of southwestern Rajasthan, Jawai (near Bera in Pali district) is a secluded wilderness gem. Despite its quiet, remote location, it is well-connected by road, rail, and air, making it easy to access for international and domestic luxury travelers alike. This guide outlines the best ways to reach our outpost from major hubs like Udaipur, Jodhpur, Mumbai, and Delhi.

1. By Air (Nearest Airports)

Jawai does not have its own airport. The nearest airports with regular domestic and international connections are:

  • ✦ Udaipur Maharana Pratap Airport (UDR): Located approximately 140 km away. The drive to the outpost takes about 2.5 to 3 hours through scenic Aravali mountain highways. This is the most popular arrival point.
  • ✦ Jodhpur Airport (JDH): Situated around 150 km away. The drive is approximately 3 hours along flat, fast highway routes.
  • ✦ Private Charter Helipad: Leopard Trails has a private helipad clearance at our reserve, allowing for direct helicopter transfers from Udaipur or Jodhpur airports.

2. By Train (Luxury Rail Access)

Traveling by train is a comfortable, scenic option. The nearest railway stations are:

  • ✦ Jawai Bandh Railway Station (JWB): Located just 15 km from the resort. Several express trains from Delhi, Mumbai, Ahmedabad, and Jaipur stop here. We provide complimentary transfers from this station for all suite bookings.
  • ✦ Falna Railway Station (FA): A major rail junction situated 35 km away, with excellent connections from Mumbai and Delhi. Private transfers take about 45 minutes.

3. By Road (Driving Routes)

The road network in Rajasthan is excellent. Key driving distances to Jawai include:

  • • From Udaipur: 140 km (2.5 hours via NH27)
  • • From Jodhpur: 150 km (3 hours via NH62)
  • • From Ahmedabad: 290 km (5.5 hours via NH48)
  • • From Jaipur: 390 km (7 hours via NH58)
