Our Green Mission

Preserving the wild harmony of Jawai for future generations.

At Leopard Trails Jawai, we believe that true luxury should be gentle on the earth. Our resort is built on a foundation of eco-responsibility and community conservation. We aim to show that high-end glamping can actively protect wildlife habitats and uplift local communities.

We operate a zero-waste, plastic-free campsite. The resort is powered entirely by a custom-built 45kW solar energy micro-grid with battery backups, ensuring a minimal carbon footprint. We also run an advanced greywater filtration system that recycles water to nourish our organic gardens and local flora.

Our Conservation Pillars

The Three Pillars of Our Program

1. Community Partnerships

Over 80% of our staff members, including tracking assistants, kitchen assistants, security guards, and maintenance crews, are hired directly from the nearby villages of Jawai. By providing training and long-term careers in sustainable tourism, we ensure that local families benefit directly from conservation.

2. Human-Wildlife Conflict Mitigation

We run a direct compensation fund for the local Rabari herdsmen. In the rare event that a leopard preys on their livestock, we provide immediate financial compensation, bypassing government administrative delays. This keeps relationships healthy and ensures the community protects leopards.

3. Wildlife Monitoring & Anti-Poaching

Our naturalists partner with local forest department officials to monitor leopard population health, document breeding caves, and support local anti-poaching watch groups. We also sponsor water holes throughout the rocky hills to support wildlife during dry summer spells.

Common Questions

Conservation FAQ

Does the resort have round-the-clock electricity?

Yes, our solar micro-grid is connected to a state-of-the-art lithium-iron battery backup system, providing reliable, clean, silent energy 24 hours a day without any noisy diesel generator interruptions.

How can guests support the conservation efforts?

Simply staying with us supports our programs, as a percentage of our profits is allocated directly to our community fund. Guests can also sponsor camera traps, support village schooling projects, or contribute directly to the Rabari livestock compensation scheme.
