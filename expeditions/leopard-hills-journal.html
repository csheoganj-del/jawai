Wildlife Case Study

Tracking the Leopardess of Jawai

An expedition journal detailing the research, scouting, lens configuration, and tracking methods used to capture close-up portraits of a resident leopardess.

✦ Guest Desire & Challenge

A professional wildlife photographer from the UK booked a 3-night stay at Leopard Trails. Their goal was highly specific: capture clear, close-up portraits of the valley\'s famous resident leopardess, known locally as "Nisha", against the stark granite kopjes during twilight. The primary challenge was Nisha\'s recent movement patterns, as she had moved her dens due to a rival male leopard entering her territory, making sightings highly unpredictable.

✦ Scout & Field Research

Our tracking naturalists launched a 48-hour scouting operation prior to the guest\'s arrival. The process involved:

  • • Pugmark Tracking: Scanned sandy crossings at dawn to locate fresh print trails.
  • • Alarm Call Audits: Consulted local Rabari shepherds regarding vocal alerts from peacocks and langur monkeys.
  • • Cave Scoping: Inspected Nisha\'s historical rock shelters using high-powered spotting scopes.

✦ Tailoring the Itinerary

Instead of driving standard tourist routes, we designed a custom tracking plan. Morning safaris were rescheduled to depart 30 minutes earlier (05:00 AM) to position the jeep before dawn at the base of Nisha\'s newly suspected cave. We coordinated with our local trackers to patrol the ridge borders, ensuring the jeep could be silently moved to the exact exit ledge where Nisha typically begins her evening hunts.

✦ Equipment & Outpost Assets Used

Having the right gear was critical for the photographer\'s twilight sessions:

  • • Custom Vehicle Setup: Open 4x4 Gypsy fitted with swiveling camera sandbag mounts to steady heavy 600mm f/4 prime lenses.
  • • Optics: Premium Leica binoculars provided to the photographer to scan the rocks in low-light conditions.
  • • Communications: Realtime walkie-talkie link between our naturalist and field trackers patrolling the valley.

✦ Experience Execution & Sighting Performance

On the second morning, our naturalist heard warning alarm calls from a langur monkey group near the north ridge. Guided by these calls, we drove the jeep to a sandy path beside the boulders. Within 20 minutes, Nisha emerged from a granite crack, stepping onto a ledge directly in front of the vehicle. Thanks to our early departure, the lighting was perfect—offering soft golden side-lighting as she sat overlooking the valley.

✦ Results & Guest Feedback

"The naturalist\'s knowledge was incredible. They predicted Nisha\'s exact movement down the rocks, allowing me to position my tripod and capture a stunning series of frames at 600mm. The custom jeep mount was a lifesaver. This is, without doubt, the finest wildlife photography experience I have had in India."

— Julian M., Professional Wildlife Photographer, UK
